# Insurance Scam Research Survey Design

## Executive Summary

This document presents a comprehensive survey design to investigate the prevalence and patterns of insurance scams across different demographic segments in India. The survey aims to collect data that will help understand how insurance fraud affects various income levels and age groups.

## Survey Objectives

### Primary Objectives
1. **Measure Prevalence**: Quantify the incidence of insurance scams across different demographics
2. **Identify Patterns**: Understand common types of scams and their distribution
3. **Demographic Analysis**: Analyze scam patterns by income level, age group, and geographic location
4. **Impact Assessment**: Measure financial and emotional impact on victims
5. **Awareness Levels**: Assess public awareness about insurance fraud

### Secondary Objectives
1. **Trust Metrics**: Evaluate trust levels in insurance industry
2. **Reporting Behavior**: Understand why scams go unreported
3. **Prevention Strategies**: Identify effective prevention measures
4. **Policy Recommendations**: Provide data-driven recommendations

## Survey Methodology

### Research Design
- **Type**: Mixed-methods approach (quantitative + qualitative)
- **Structure**: Multi-stage stratified sampling
- **Data Collection**: Online + offline modes
- **Duration**: 6-month longitudinal study

### Target Population
- **Geographic Coverage**: Pan-India (urban and rural)
- **Age Range**: 18-65 years
- **Income Levels**: All income segments (below poverty line to high income)
- **Insurance Status**: Policyholders and non-policyholders

## Sample Design

### Sample Size Calculation
- **Total Sample Size**: 15,000 respondents
- **Margin of Error**: ±3% at 95% confidence level
- **Response Rate**: Expected 70% completion rate

### Stratification Strategy

#### By Income Level (5 tiers)
1. **Tier 1**: Below Poverty Line (< ₹1.5 lakh annually)
   - Sample: 3,000 respondents (20%)
   
2. **Tier 2**: Low Income (₹1.5-5 lakh annually)
   - Sample: 4,500 respondents (30%)
   
3. **Tier 3**: Middle Income (₹5-15 lakh annually)
   - Sample: 4,500 respondents (30%)
   
4. **Tier 4**: Upper Middle Income (₹15-50 lakh annually)
   - Sample: 2,250 respondents (15%)
   
5. **Tier 5**: High Income (> ₹50 lakh annually)
   - Sample: 750 respondents (5%)

#### By Age Groups (4 categories)
1. **Young Adults**: 18-30 years (25% - 3,750)
2. **Early Career**: 31-45 years (35% - 5,250)
3. **Mid-Career**: 46-55 years (25% - 3,750)
4. **Pre-retirement**: 56-65 years (15% - 2,250)

#### By Geographic Distribution
- **Urban**: 60% (9,000 respondents)
- **Rural**: 40% (6,000 respondents)

#### By States/UTs
- **Large States**: 500-800 respondents each
- **Medium States**: 300-500 respondents each
- **Small States/UTs**: 100-300 respondents each

## Survey Instrument Design

### Section 1: Screening and Demographics (10 questions)

#### Screening Questions
1. **Age Verification**: "What is your current age?" [Open-ended]
2. **Residence**: "In which state/UT do you currently reside?" [Dropdown]
3. **Consent**: "Do you consent to participate in this survey?" [Yes/No]

#### Demographic Profile
4. **Gender**: [Male/Female/Other/Prefer not to say]
5. **Marital Status**: [Single/Married/Divorced/Widowed]
6. **Education Level**: 
   - No formal education
   - Primary (1-5)
   - Secondary (6-10)
   - Higher Secondary (11-12)
   - Graduate
   - Post-graduate
   - Professional degree

7. **Occupation**: [Dropdown with 20+ options + Other]
8. **Annual Household Income**: 
   - Below ₹1.5 lakh
   - ₹1.5-5 lakh
   - ₹5-15 lakh
   - ₹15-50 lakh
   - Above ₹50 lakh
   - Prefer not to disclose

9. **Location Type**: [Urban/Rural/Semi-urban]
10. **Insurance Status**: "Do you currently hold any insurance policy?" [Yes/No]

### Section 2: Insurance Awareness and Experience (15 questions)

#### Insurance Knowledge Assessment
11. **Policy Types**: "Which types of insurance are you familiar with?" [Multiple choice]
    - Life insurance
    - Health insurance
    - Motor insurance
    - Property insurance
    - Crop insurance
    - Other

12. **Current Policies**: "Which insurance policies do you currently hold?" [Multiple choice]

13. **Premium Spending**: "How much do you spend annually on insurance premiums?"
    - Less than ₹10,000
    - ₹10,000-50,000
    - ₹50,000-1,00,000
    - More than ₹1,00,000
    - Don't know

#### Past Experiences
14. **Claim History**: "Have you ever filed an insurance claim?" [Yes/No]
15. **Claim Success**: "Were your claims approved without issues?" [Yes/No/Partially]
16. **Claim Challenges**: "What challenges did you face during claim process?" [Multiple choice]

### Section 3: Scam Experience and Impact (20 questions)

#### Personal Scam Experience
17. **Scam Experience**: "Have you or anyone you know experienced an insurance scam?" [Yes/No/Unsure]
18. **Scam Type**: "What type of scam was it?" [Multiple choice]
    - Fake policy sale
    - Premium fraud
    - Claim manipulation
    - Identity theft
    - Agent fraud
    - Other

19. **Scam Amount**: "What was the approximate financial loss?"
    - Less than ₹10,000
    - ₹10,000-50,000
    - ₹50,000-1,00,000
    - ₹1,00,000-5,00,000
    - More than ₹5,00,000
    - Don't want to disclose

20. **Scam Channel**: "How did the scam occur?" [Multiple choice]
    - Online/Internet
    - Phone call
    - In-person by agent
    - Through friend/relative
    - Fake website/app
    - Other

21. **Reporting Behavior**: "Did you report the scam?" [Yes/No]
22. **Non-reporting Reasons**: "Why didn't you report?" [If No to Q21]
    - Didn't know how to report
    - Thought it was useless
    - Fear of retaliation
    - Too complicated
    - Other

#### Impact Assessment
23. **Financial Impact**: "How severe was the financial impact?" [Scale 1-5]
24. **Emotional Impact**: "How did the scam affect you emotionally?" [Multiple choice]
25. **Trust Impact**: "How has your trust in insurance companies changed?" [Scale 1-5]
26. **Behavior Change**: "Did you change your insurance behavior after the scam?" [Yes/No]

### Section 4: Awareness and Prevention (15 questions)

#### Fraud Awareness
27. **Fraud Types**: "Which types of insurance fraud are you aware of?" [Multiple choice]
28. **Prevention Knowledge**: "Do you know how to protect yourself from insurance scams?" [Yes/No]
29. **Information Sources**: "Where do you get information about insurance fraud?" [Multiple choice]

#### Prevention Measures
30. **Verification Methods**: "How do you verify insurance policies/agents?" [Multiple choice]
31. **Red Flags**: "What warning signs do you look for?" [Multiple choice]
32. **Precautionary Actions**: "What precautions do you take?" [Multiple choice]

#### Institutional Trust
33. **Company Trust**: "How much do you trust insurance companies?" [Scale 1-5]
34. **Regulator Trust**: "How much do you trust IRDAI?" [Scale 1-5]
35. **Agent Trust**: "How much do you trust insurance agents?" [Scale 1-5]

### Section 5: Open Feedback (5 questions)

36. **Suggestions**: "What suggestions do you have to prevent insurance scams?" [Open-ended]
37. **Government Role**: "What should the government do to protect consumers?" [Open-ended]
38. **Company Responsibility**: "What should insurance companies do?" [Open-ended]
39. **Personal Experience**: "Please share any personal experience with insurance fraud" [Optional]
40. **Additional Comments**: "Any other comments or suggestions?" [Optional]

## Data Collection Methodology

### Online Survey Platform
- **Platform**: Qualtrics/SurveyMonkey
- **Mobile Optimization**: Responsive design
- **Offline Capability**: Downloadable for offline areas
- **Language Options**: English, Hindi, and regional languages

### Offline Data Collection
- **Field Teams**: Trained survey enumerators
- **Paper Forms**: For areas with limited digital access
- **Tablets**: For digital data entry in remote areas
- **Quality Control**: Real-time validation and checks

### Quality Assurance Measures
1. **Pilot Testing**: 500-respondent pilot study
2. **Enumerator Training**: Comprehensive training program
3. **Data Validation**: Real-time and post-collection validation
4. **Back-checking**: 10% random sample verification
5. **Consistency Checks**: Cross-validation of responses

## Data Analysis Plan

### Quantitative Analysis
- **Descriptive Statistics**: Frequency distributions, means, medians
- **Cross-tabulation**: Demographic breakdowns
- **Correlation Analysis**: Relationships between variables
- **Regression Analysis**: Predictive modeling
- **Cluster Analysis**: Identify victim profiles

### Qualitative Analysis
- **Thematic Analysis**: Open-ended responses
- **Content Analysis**: Fraud patterns and narratives
- **Sentiment Analysis**: Emotional impact assessment
- **Text Mining**: Key themes and patterns

### Key Performance Indicators
1. **Scam Prevalence Rate**: % of population affected
2. **Average Financial Loss**: Mean/median loss amounts
3. **Reporting Rate**: % of scams reported
4. **Awareness Index**: Composite awareness score
5. **Trust Index**: Composite trust score
